Grocery Store Inventory & Sales Management

SDG Alignment:
SDG 2 – Zero Hunger
SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption & Production
SDG 8 – Decent Work & Economic Growth

Problem:
Grocery stores face:
- Difficulty tracking product inventory efficiently
- Products expiring before being sold
- Overstocking or stockouts due to poor replenishment planning
- Long checkout queues affecting customer experience
- Need for dynamic product display sorted by price, rating, or expiry

The system must:
- Track inventory in real-time
- Identify best-selling and soon-to-expire products
- Optimize stock arrangement
- Manage checkout lines efficiently
- Display products sorted by price, rating, or expiry

Implementation Steps (ADS):
Product Catalog & Lookup:
Store all products, categories, and details (price, SKU, quantity, expiry) in a hash table for fast access.

Best-Selling & Soon-to-Expire Products Tracking:
Use heaps:
- Max-Heap for best-selling products
- Min-Heap for products nearing expiry
This helps dynamically identify which products to promote or monitor.

Shelf Arrangement & Product Display:
Sort products according to different criteria:
- Expiry Date (soon-to-expire first): QuickSort
- Price (low to high / high to low): MergeSort
- Customer Ratings (high to low): HeapSort
This ensures organized shelves and easier customer browsing.

Checkout Queue Management:
Use a queue (FIFO) to manage customers at checkout counters.
Ensures fair and orderly service.

Healthcare Appointment Scheduling & Emergency Dispatch

SDG Alignment:
SDG 3 – Good Health & Well-being
SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities & Communities

Problem:
Patients face long wait times, delayed ambulance response, and inefficient doctor appointment management. Hospitals need a system that:
- Prioritizes emergency patients
- Reduces travel time for ambulances
- Optimizes doctor schedules
- Provides fast access to patient records

Implementation Steps (ADS):
City & Hospital Graph Representation:
Represent hospitals, clinics, and patient homes as nodes in a weighted graph.
Edges represent roads; weights store travel time between locations.

Patient Records Management:
Store all patient records, appointment history, and medical data in a hash table.
This allows O(1) access to patient information during scheduling and emergency handling.

Appointment Queue Management:
Use a priority queue (min-heap) to manage patient appointments.
Patients with urgent cases are given higher priority to reduce wait times.

Ambulance Route Optimization:
Use Dijkstra’s algorithm on the weighted graph to calculate the fastest path for ambulances.
Min-heap is used in Dijkstra to efficiently select the next node with minimum travel time.

Doctor Scheduling Optimization:
Apply sorting algorithms to arrange patient appointments by urgency and availability.
Use arrays or hash tables to track doctor availability and appointment slots.
This minimizes idle time for doctors and reduces patient wait times.

Emergency Handling & Updates:
Continuously update patient queues and ambulance routes based on new emergencies.
Use priority queues to dynamically reorder patients or ambulance dispatch as needed.

Restaurant Order & Delivery System

SDG Alignment:
SDG 8 – Decent Work & Economic Growth
SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities & Communities
SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption & Production

Problem:
Restaurants face challenges such as:
- Managing multiple customer orders efficiently
- Ensuring fast and timely delivery
- Recommending popular or personalized dishes
- Tracking trending menu items and combos
- Handling order history for repeat customers

The system must:
- Process orders in the order they are received
- Optimize delivery routes for multiple locations
- Track customer preferences and order history
- Suggest popular or recommended dishes dynamically

Implementation Steps (ADS):
Order Management:
Use a queue (FIFO) to manage customer orders.
Ensures orders are processed in the order they are received.

Menu & Customer Preferences:
Store menu items, customer preferences, and past orders in hash tables for fast retrieval.

Trending Dishes & Popular Combos:
Use heaps to dynamically track best-selling or trending menu items.

Delivery Route Optimization:
Model delivery locations and routes as a weighted graph.
Apply Dijkstra’s algorithm to determine the fastest delivery paths.

Dish Recommendation:
Maintain a list of dishes with their ratings and frequency of orders.
Apply MergeSort to sort dishes by popularity or rating in descending order.
Suggest the top-N dishes to the customer based on sorted list.
Sorting ensures that highly rated and frequently ordered dishes are prioritized.
